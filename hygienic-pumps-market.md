<p><strong>The Hygienic Pumps Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Centrifugal Type</li><li>Positive Displacement</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hygienic pumps are widely used in industries where maintaining sanitary conditions is crucial, such as food and beverage, pharmaceutical, and biotechnology. The market for hygienic pumps is broadly categorized into three types. </p><p>1. Centrifugal Type: These pumps use centrifugal force to transfer liquids and have high efficiency, making them suitable for applications involving low viscosity fluids or clean-in-place (CIP) processes.</p><p>2. Positive Displacement: These pumps create pressure by trapping and transferring a fixed amount of fluid per revolution. They are ideal for applications that require precise and gentle handling of viscous or shear-sensitive fluids.</p><p>3. Other Market: This category includes specialized hygienic pumps with unique features for specific applications. Examples include peristaltic pumps for accurate metering, air-operated diaphragm pumps for hygienic and sterile transfer, and lobe pumps for gentle and low-shear pumping of liquid and viscous products.</p><p>Overall, hygienic pumps play a vital role in maintaining cleanliness and ensuring the safe transfer of fluids in various industries.</p></p>
