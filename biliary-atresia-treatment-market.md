<p><p>Biliary atresia treatment refers to the medical management and surgical interventions provided to individuals suffering from biliary atresia, a rare liver disease characterized by the obstruction or absence of the bile ducts outside or inside the liver. The aim of treatment is to restore bile flow and prevent further liver damage.</p><p>The treatment of biliary atresia typically involves a surgical procedure called the Kasai procedure or hepatoportoenterostomy, which involves removing the blocked bile ducts and creating a connection between the liver and the small intestine. This procedure helps to restore the bile flow and prevent the bile from accumulating in the liver, thereby preserving liver function.</p><p>In cases where the Kasai procedure is not successful or if liver damage is severe, liver transplantation may be necessary. Liver transplantation offers the best chance of long-term survival for individuals with biliary atresia.</p><p>The global biliary atresia treatment market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
