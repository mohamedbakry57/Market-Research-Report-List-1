<p><p>Rosin resin is categorized into three main types: gum resin, wood resin, and tall oil resin. Gum resin is derived from the exudation of certain trees, such as pine trees, and is often used in the production of adhesives, insulating materials, and paints. Wood resin, on the other hand, is extracted from wood sources and is commonly employed in the manufacturing of varnishes, lacquers, and coatings. Lastly, tall oil resin is obtained during the process of tall oil production, which is a byproduct of the paper manufacturing industry, and is utilized in the formulation of inks, soaps, and more.</p></p>
