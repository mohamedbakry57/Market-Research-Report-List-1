<p><p>Vitreous detachment treatment refers to medical interventions aimed at managing the condition wherein the vitreous gel in the eye becomes partially separated from the retina. In most cases, vitreous detachment is a normal occurrence that happens as people age. While it might not require any treatment, some cases might lead to complications such as retinal tears or detachments, which can potentially cause vision loss if left untreated. Therefore, certain treatment options are available to address these complications.</p><p>One conventional treatment is laser therapy, which involves using a high-energy laser to create small burns around the area of the retina at risk of tearing. This helps to create scarring, which then seals the retina and prevents further detachment. Another common treatment option is cryotherapy, where freezing probes are used to create scar tissue around the retinal tears or detachments to secure them in place.</p><p>The vitreous detachment treatment market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years.
