<p><p>Glass fibers are classified into two main market types: general-purpose and special-purpose. General-purpose glass fibers are commonly used in various applications, such as construction, automotive, and electronics industries. They possess excellent strength, flexibility, and resistance to heat and chemicals, making them suitable for a wide range of applications. On the other hand, special-purpose glass fibers are designed for specific applications, such as high-performance composites, insulation material, optical fibers, and filtration systems. These fibers are tailored to meet specific requirements in terms of strength, durability, conductivity, and other properties.</p></p>
