<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Portable Ophthalmic Ultrasound Systems Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>A- Scan</li><li>B- Scan</li><li>Combined Scan</li><li>Pachymeter</li><li>Ultrasound Bio-microscopy (UBM)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Portable ophthalmic ultrasound systems are used for various diagnostic procedures in ophthalmology. The A-scan uses ultrasound waves to measure axial length, allowing for accurate intraocular lens calculations during cataract surgery. B-scan provides cross-sectional images of the eye, helping to detect abnormalities in the posterior segment. Combined scan combines A-scan and B-scan functionalities. Pachymeter measures corneal thickness, crucial for glaucoma diagnosis and monitoring. Ultrasound biomicroscopy (UBM) provides high-resolution images of the anterior segment, helping diagnose conditions like angle-closure glaucoma. These portable systems offer convenience and versatility for ophthalmic diagnostics.</p></p>
