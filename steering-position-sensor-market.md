It provides information to the vehicle's electronic control unit (ECU), enabling it to adjust various functions such as power steering, stability control, and automated parking systems. The sensor usually consists of a potentiometer or a rotary encoder, which detects the movement of the steering wheel and sends corresponding signals to the ECU.</p><p>The steering position sensor market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years. <p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The steering position sensor market consists of two main types: magnetic sensor and contacting sensor. Magnetic sensors use magnetic fields to measure the angle and position of the steering wheel, providing accurate data to the vehicle's control system. Contacting sensors, on the other hand, make physical contact with the steering shaft, detecting the direction and rotation of the wheel. Both types serve the same purpose of providing crucial steering information to the vehicle's systems, allowing for safe and efficient operation.</p></p>
