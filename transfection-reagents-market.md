<p><p>Transfection reagents are used to introduce foreign genetic material into cells for various research purposes. Plasmid transfection focuses on transferring plasmids (small DNA molecules) into cells to express a specific gene. SiRNA transfection employs small interfering RNA molecules to silence specific genes by inhibiting their expression. Other types of transfection reagents refer to methods that use different genetic materials or techniques for introducing foreign DNA/RNA into cells. These categories represent different approaches in genetic research, enabling scientists to study gene functions and develop potential therapeutic strategies.</p></p>
