<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) is a technique used to measure the amount of target DNA or RNA in a sample. Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR is a type of dPCR where samples are divided into thousands of tiny droplets to enable parallel PCR reactions. This method offers high throughput and increased sensitivity. The Droplet Digital PCR market refers to the market for dPCR systems that use droplets instead of microfluidic chips. These systems can provide absolute quantification of target molecules, making them advantageous for applications such as cancer research and genetic testing.</p></p>
