<p><p>An integrated circuit type optocoupler, also known as optoisolator or photocoupler, is a device that electronically connects two separate circuits using light. It consists of a light-emitting diode (LED) and a phototransistor or a photodiode, which are enclosed in a single package. The LED emits light when an electric current passes through it, and this light activates the phototransistor or photodiode, allowing it to conduct current and transmit the electrical signal from one circuit to another.</p><p>The future of the integrated circuit type optocoupler market looks promising. On the other hand, Linear Optocouplers are used for DC signal transmission and are known for their accurate linear transfer characteristics.
