<p><p>The Fluorescent Chloride Sensor market consists of two main types: Quinoline-Based Chloride and Forster Resonance Energy Transfer (FRET) Based Chloride. Quinoline-Based Chloride sensors use a quinoline-based compound to detect chloride ions. These sensors emit fluorescence when they come into contact with chloride ions, allowing their measurement. On the other hand, FRET-Based Chloride sensors use a phenomenon called Forster Resonance Energy Transfer to measure chloride ions. These sensors work by transferring energy between two different fluorescent molecules when chloride ions are present, leading to a change in fluorescence intensity.</p></p>
