<p><p>Orthodontic screws, also known as mini-screws or temporary anchorage devices (TADs), are small titanium or stainless steel screws that are used to provide additional support and stability in orthodontic treatments. These screws are inserted into the bone, typically in areas where dental implants are not feasible, to serve as an anchor for orthodontic appliances.</p><p>The global orthodontic screw market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of malocclusion and the growing demand for aesthetic orthodontic treatments are driving the market growth. Orthodontic screws offer several advantages such as reduced treatment time, enhanced treatment efficiency, and improved patient compliance, which further contribute to market growth.</p><p>In addition, technological advancements in orthodontic screws, such as the development of self-drilling and self-tapping screws, are boosting their adoption. These advanced screws eliminate the need for pre-drilling or tapping, simplifying the placement procedure and reducing treatment time.</p><p>Moreover, the rising geriatric population and the increasing number of orthodontic procedures are expected to fuel market growth. The elderly population is more prone to dental misalignment, leading to the need for orthodontic correction. Furthermore, the growing demand for invisible orthodontic appliances, such as clear aligners, is creating lucrative opportunities for the orthodontic screw market.</p><p>However, limited reimbursement policies for orthodontic treatments and the high cost associated with these procedures could hinder market growth to some extent.</p><p>Overall, the orthodontic screw market is expected to experience a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period due to favorable market trends, technological advancements, and increasing demand for orthodontic treatments.</p></p>
