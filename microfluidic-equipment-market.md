These devices find applications in various fields, including biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, healthcare, chemistry, and diagnostics, due to their ability to perform precise and rapid analysis of biological and chemical samples.</p><p>The global microfluidic equipment market is projected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>The microfluidic equipment market can be categorized into three types: glass, polymer, and silicon. Glass-based microfluidic equipment utilizes glass as a substrate, offering excellent optical properties and biocompatibility. Polymer-based microfluidic equipment uses polymers such as polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) and polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), providing flexibility, easy fabrication, and cost-effectiveness. Silicon-based microfluidic equipment employs silicon as a substrate, enabling high fabrication precision and compatibility with integrated electronics. These different types cater to diverse applications and requirements in the field of microfluidics, ranging from biomedical research and diagnosis to chemical analysis and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
