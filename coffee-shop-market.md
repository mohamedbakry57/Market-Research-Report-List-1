<p><p>There are two common types of coffee shop markets: franchise and chain markets. In a franchise market, independent coffee shop owners buy the rights to operate under an established brand, benefiting from the brand's reputation and support. Franchisees must adhere to specific guidelines set by the franchisor. In a chain market, multiple coffee shops are owned and operated by a single company, using the same brand and following standardized procedures across all locations. Chains often have more control over quality and consistency but may lack the individuality of independent shops.</p></p>
