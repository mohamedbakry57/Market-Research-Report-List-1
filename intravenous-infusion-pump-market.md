<p><p>The Intravenous Infusion Pump market is segmented into several types. Volumetric Infusion Pumps are commonly used for delivering large volumes of fluids accurately. Syringe Infusion Pumps allow precise delivery of small volumes, ideal for medications with low dosage requirements. Insulin Infusion Pumps are specifically designed for diabetic patients to administer insulin continuously. Enteral Infusion Pumps are used for delivering nutrition directly into the digestive system. Ambulatory Infusion Pumps enable patients to receive continuous infusion therapy while being mobile. Patient-Controlled Analgesia Infusion Pumps allow patients to self-administer pain medication. Implantable Infusion Pumps are surgically placed devices that deliver medications directly to a specific area. Others include specialized infusion pumps for specific applications.</p></p>
