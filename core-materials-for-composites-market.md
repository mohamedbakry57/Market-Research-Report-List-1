<p><p>The core materials used in composites can be categorized into two main types: foam cores and honeycombs. Foam cores are lightweight materials, typically made from various types of foams, that provide strength and stability to composites while reducing their overall weight. On the other hand, honeycombs are structures made up of cells that are bonded together, forming a strong and lightweight core material. These different core materials offer unique advantages and are commonly used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and construction to enhance the properties of composite materials.</p></p>
