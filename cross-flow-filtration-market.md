<p><p>Cross flow filtration is a filtration technique that separates particles from a liquid by passing it through a porous membrane or set of membranes. Unlike traditional filtration methods where the liquid flows perpendicular to the filter, in cross flow filtration, the liquid flows parallel to the filter, creating a shearing effect that prevents clogging.</p><p>The cross flow filtration market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. The market is being driven by the increasing demand for efficient filtration systems in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food and beverages, biotechnology, and water treatment.
