<p><ul><li>Diode Based Attenuators</li><li>MMIC Based Attenuators</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Variable attenuators are electronic devices used to control the level of a signal passing through a circuit. There are different types of variable attenuators available in the market. Diode-based attenuators use PIN diodes to vary the attenuation level, allowing for precise control over signal strength. On the other hand, MMIC-based attenuators utilize Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuit technology to provide high-performance attenuation. Both types of attenuators are commonly used in various applications such as telecommunications, wireless communication systems, and test equipment to control signal strength and improve overall system performance.</p></p>
