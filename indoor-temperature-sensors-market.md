Wired indoor temperature sensors require physical connections to the monitoring system or the central control unit, ensuring stable and reliable data transmission. On the other hand, wireless indoor temperature sensors utilize wireless communication technologies like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth to transmit temperature data, providing more flexibility in terms of installation and accessibility.
