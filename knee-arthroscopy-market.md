<p><p>Knee arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure that allows orthopedic surgeons to visualize, diagnose, and treat problems inside the knee joint. It involves inserting a small camera, called an arthroscope, through a small incision in the knee. The arthroscope transmits images to a video monitor, guiding the surgeon to perform necessary repairs or corrections.</p><p>The knee arthroscopy market has been witnessing significant growth due to various factors such as the rising prevalence of knee injuries and disorders, growing geriatric population, and increasing adoption of minimally invasive surgeries. Additionally, advancements in technology, such as high-definition cameras and 3D arthroscopy systems, have improved the quality and accuracy of knee arthroscopy procedures.</p><p>Moreover, the market is driven by the growing demand for outpatient surgeries and shorter hospital stays, reducing healthcare costs and patient recovery time. The increased focus on sports medicine and orthopedic research and development has also contributed to market growth.</p><p>Key trends in the knee arthroscopy market include the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and robotics in surgical procedures, which aims to improve surgical precision and outcomes.
