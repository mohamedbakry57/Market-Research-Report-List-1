<p><p>Battery charger controllers can be classified into two types: linear and non-linear. </p><p>Linear battery charger controllers regulate the charging process by directly controlling the voltage and current supplied to the battery. They provide a simple and cost-effective solution, but they have limited efficiency and may generate excess heat during charging.</p><p>On the other hand, non-linear battery charger controllers employ more advanced techniques, such as pulse-width modulation (PWM) or switching regulators, to efficiently regulate the charging process. They offer higher efficiency and better control over charging parameters but generally come at a higher cost.</p></p>
