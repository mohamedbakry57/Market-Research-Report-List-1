<p><p>Medical sterile packaging technology refers to the methods used to maintain the sterility of medical products and equipment during storage and transportation. Chemical sterilization involves using sterilizing agents such as ethylene oxide or hydrogen peroxide to kill microorganisms. Physical sterilization, on the other hand, utilizes heat or radiation to eliminate pathogens. Both these methods are crucial in preventing contamination and ensuring the safety and efficacy of medical devices. The market for these sterilization technologies is driven by the increasing demand for sterile packaging in healthcare settings.</p></p>
