<p><p>An interbody cage, also known as a spinal cage, is a medical device used in spinal fusion surgeries to treat various spinal conditions such as degenerative disc disease, herniated disc, or spondylolisthesis. It is a hollow, cylindrical device made of biocompatible materials like titanium or polyetheretherketone (PEEK), and is inserted between the vertebral bodies to restore disc height, decompress nerve roots, and promote spinal fusion.</p><p>The interbody cage market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. Several factors contribute to this market growth, including the rising prevalence of degenerative disc diseases and spinal disorders, an aging population, technological advancements in interbody cage design, and increasing adoption of minimally invasive procedures. These include the development of customizable interbody cages specific to patient anatomy, the integration of 3D printing technology for precise implant designs, and the introduction of expandable and bioactive cages for better clinical outcomes. Furthermore, the use of biocompatible materials, such as PEEK, is gaining prominence due to their favorable properties like radiolucency and modulus of elasticity similar to bone.</p><p>In conclusion, the interbody cage market is witnessing strong growth and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 13.4% during the forecast period. <p><p>The interbody cage market consists of two main types: metal type and polymeric type. The metal type involves cages made from metals such as titanium or stainless steel, which provide strength and stability to the spine. These cages are durable and can withstand high loads during the fusion process. On the other hand, the polymeric type involves cages made from non-metal materials, such as polymers or biocompatible plastics, which offer flexibility and a natural bone-like structure. They provide a favorable environment for bone growth and have less risk of stress shielding.</p></p>
