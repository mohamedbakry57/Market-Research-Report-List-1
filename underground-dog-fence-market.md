<p><p>An underground dog fence, also known as an invisible fence or electric dog fence, is a system used to keep dogs within a certain boundary without the need for physical fences. It consists of an underground wire that is buried around the perimeter of the desired area and a collar worn by the dog. The wire emits radio signals, and if the dog crosses the boundary, it receives a mild electric shock through the collar, serving as a deterrent.</p><p>The global underground dog fence market is expected to experience substantial growth during the forecast period of 2021 to 2026. The increasing adoption of dogs as pets worldwide is one of the key factors driving the market growth. With the growing population of dogs, the need for containment solutions such as underground dog fences has also increased.</p><p>Additionally, the advancements in technology have led to the development of more efficient and user-friendly underground dog fence systems. These systems now come with features like wireless connectivity, smartphone compatibility, and multi-dog support, which have further boosted their demand.</p><p>Furthermore, the rise in urbanization and the shrinking size of yards in residential areas have made traditional physical fences less feasible. This has led pet owners to opt for underground dog fences as a more efficient and convenient solution.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a trend of manufacturers focusing on creating more eco-friendly and humane alternatives to the traditional electric shock collars. This includes using vibration or sound-based deterrents, which are considered to be gentler on dogs while still effectively keeping them within the boundaries.</p><p>Overall, with the increasing pet ownership and technological advancements, the underground dog fence market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The underground dog fence market includes two main types: wired-fence and wireless fence. Wired-fence systems consist of a buried wire that creates a boundary and transmits signals to a collar worn by the dog. These systems offer precise and customizable boundaries but require installation. On the other hand, wireless fence systems use radio signals to define a boundary without the need for buried wires. They provide convenience and easy setup but may have signal limitations and less customization options compared to the wired-fence systems.</p></p>
