<p><p>Elastography is a medical imaging technology used to assess the stiffness or elasticity of tissues. The market for elastography comprises various types, including ultrasound elastography, magnetic resonance elastography (MRE), and others. Ultrasound elastography utilizes ultrasound waves to measure tissue elasticity, providing real-time imaging. MRE, on the other hand, uses magnetic resonance imaging to generate detailed maps of tissue stiffness. Other elastography types may include shear wave elastography and acoustic radiation force impulse imaging. These various techniques offer non-invasive and accurate ways to diagnose and monitor diseases affecting tissue elasticity.</p></p>
