<p><p>Zika virus is a flavivirus primarily transmitted through Aedes mosquitoes, which also carry dengue and chikungunya viruses. Initially identified in Uganda in 1947, it attracted global attention during the 2015-2016 outbreak in South and Central America, raising concerns due to its potential association with certain birth defects, notably microcephaly. The symptoms of Zika virus infection are generally mild, including fever, rash, conjunctivitis, muscle and joint pain, and headache, and typically last for up to a week. However, the major concern lies in the impact of Zika infection during pregnancy, as it has been linked to severe congenital brain abnormalities in newborns.</p><p>The Zika virus market has experienced significant growth due to increased public health awareness, rising incidence of Zika virus infections, and the absence of specific antiviral treatment options. Serologic tests, on the other hand, detect antibodies produced by the body in response to the virus. These tests are used to determine past infections or to assess the immune status of individuals.
