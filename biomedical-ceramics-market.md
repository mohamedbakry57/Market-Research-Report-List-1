<p><p>Biomedical ceramics refer to a group of materials used in the medical field for various applications. The market for these materials is classified into three main types: bioinert ceramics, bioactive ceramics, and bioresorbable ceramics. Bioinert ceramics are biocompatible and do not elicit any response in the body. Bioactive ceramics bond with living tissues and promote regeneration. Bioresorbable ceramics are designed to degrade over time, allowing the body to absorb them. All three types have unique properties and offer different advantages for medical treatments and applications.</p></p>
