<p><p>Dental handpieces are essential tools used by dentists during several dental procedures such as polishing, cutting, and shaping teeth. Dental handpieces can be air-driven, electric, or hybrid, each serving its specific purpose in dentistry.</p><p>The dental handpieces market is witnessing significant growth due to the growing prevalence of dental diseases and increasing dental procedures globally. Technological advancements in dental handpieces, such as the introduction of electric handpieces that offer higher torque and precision, are driving market growth. Additionally, the rise in dental tourism, increasing disposable income, and a growing geriatric population with dental issues further contribute to market growth.</p><p>The market is also influenced by various trends. One significant trend is the shift from traditional air-driven handpieces to electric handpieces. <p><p>The dental handpieces market consists of three main types: air-driven handpieces, electric handpieces, and hybrid air-electric handpieces. Air-driven handpieces utilize compressed air to generate the rotating motion for drilling. Electric handpieces utilize a motor to generate the rotation, providing consistent and precise control. Hybrid air-electric handpieces combine both air and electric power, offering the benefits of both types. Each type has its advantages and is chosen based on the dentist's preference, procedure type, and specific requirements.</p></p>
