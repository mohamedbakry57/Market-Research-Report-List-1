<p><p>Biogas scrubbing systems are utilized to remove impurities from biogas before it can be utilized as a clean energy source. Wet chemical scrubbers use a liquid chemical to absorb contaminants, while classic biological scrubbers employ microbial activity to metabolize and convert impurities into harmless byproducts. Biological H2S scrubbers specifically target and remove hydrogen sulfide, a toxic gas commonly present in biogas. Other types of biogas scrubbing systems include physical scrubbers, membrane scrubbers, and dry chemical scrubbers, each employing distinct methods to ensure the purification of biogas.</p></p>
