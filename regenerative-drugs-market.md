<p><p>Regenerative drugs can be categorized into four types based on their composition. The first type is synthetic material, which is artificially created to mimic natural tissues and promote regrowth. The second type is biologically derived material, which is derived from animal or human cells to enhance tissue repair. The third type is genetically engineered material, where genes are altered to aid regeneration. Finally, there are other types that include unique compositions not fitting into the previous three categories. These classifications help in understanding and developing treatments for various regenerative medicine applications.</p></p>
